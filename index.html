PM Johnson gets ready to bring Brexit deal to Parliament .

    The Facts Today:

  • A Brexit deal has been agreed on by the EU28. PM Johnson has called it a ‘great new deal’, Jean-Claude Juncker has called it a ‘fair and balanced argument’.

  • PM Johnson has committed to a free trade agreement with the EU with no tariffs.

  • The deal will be voted on in Parliament on Saturday.

  • It will be opposed by the DUP, the Scottish National Party(Sturgeon - SNP MPs will not vote for Brexit in any form). Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn has said that the ‘deal should be rejected’, as he says it would involve ‘triggering a race to the bottom’ with regard to workers’ rights and environmental protections.

  • He made these comments nearly 30 minutes before the legal text had been released.

  • PM Johnson will need Labour MPs and the 21 former Conservative MPs he expelled to vote for the deal for it to pass.

  • Under the deal ,Northern Ireland will maintain EU custom standards, so that checks can be done at the Irish Sea, thereby removing the need for a hard border in Ireland.

‘Pause’ called on the Turkey offensive in Syria

    The Facts Today:

  • Thursday - Turkey’s Foreign Minister Mevlut Cavusolga announced a ‘pause’ on Operation Peace Spring - “We are suspending the operation, not halting it”.

  • He has made it clear this is to allow for a Kurdish withdrawal.

  • American Vice-President Mike Pence and Cavusolga have said the ‘pause’ is to be for 5 days.

  • Since the offensive began last week, at least 160,000 people have been displaced.

  • Turkey has defended the mission, wanting to use the cleared border region to settle 3.6 million Syrian refugees.

Next years G7 summit to be held in President Trump’s resort in Florida - climate change won't be discussed.

    The Facts Today:

  • The White House has chosen President Trump’s golf resort in Florida for June’s G7 summit.

  • The acting White House Chief of Staff Mick Mulvaney has said that the event will be held ‘at cost’.

  • President Trump has stepped down from running his company, but he still has ownership of his business. His sons are now involved in managing them.

Protests in Lebanon over increased taxes.

    The Facts Today:

  • Hundreds marched in Lebanon as tensions simmering since an austerity budget in July reached a point.

  • This is the second round of protests in a month. Schools are to be closed on Friday due to the protests.

  • The government has declared a state of ‘economic emergency’. Lebanon is one of the most indebted countries in the world.

  • The protests come as it was announced the government is planning on raising VAT by 4% over the next 2 years, and putting a 20c charge on WhatsApp calls(although this has been scrapped).

  • The government is hoping that these plans would enable to country to avail of an $11bn international aid package.

  • These protests come as 3700 acres of forestry have been burned due to over 200 wildfires. Local experts blame an ‘abandoned’ local infrastructure.
