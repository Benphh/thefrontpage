Letwin Amendment forces extension request and vote delay for PM Johnson.

    The Facts Today:

  • Earlier today(Saturday) the planned vote in the British Parliament on PM Johnsons’s Brexit deal was cancelled after a separate amendment allowed for the delay of the vote.

  • The amendment was tabled by Oliver Letwin, a formerly Conservative MP who was removed from the party by the PM, along with 21 others, as they voted for the Benn Act, which made a No-Deal Brexit illegal.

  • If the UK government was to ask for an extension, it had to be by Saturday. The introduction of the Benn Act meant that PM Johnson was forced to send a letter to the EU asking for an extension until the 31 January 2020.

  • PM Johnson sent a letter to the European Council members requesting an extension, but also making it clear he personally did not want one. It is expected that they will allow an extension.

  • It is now expected that a vote on the deal will take place in Parliament on Monday. PM Johnson still aims to exit the EU on the 31 October.

The Brexit Withdrawal Agreement.

    The Facts Today:

  • Under the Agreement, Northern Ireland will obey some EU rules like laws for vetinary controls, agricultural production and VAT on goods. These measures mean there is nor border on the island of Ireland, and instead custom checks will be at the Irish Sea.

  • The agreement will include a mechanism for multi-party consent on the issue from Stormont after two years.

  • PM Johnson has said that his aim is for an eventual free trade agreement with Europe, with no tariffs - something Britain had inside the EU.

  • The Labour Party are opposed to the bill on the basis that enviromental and worker protections have been moved to the political declaration of the agreement - which can be thought of as future intentions, but not the current rules.

5 dead after twin attacks in Burkina Faso.

    The Facts Today:

  • 4 soldiers and a policeman have been killed in two simultaneous attacks on army bases in Burkina Faso, according to a statement released by the army. 11 soldiers were also injured.

  • The army did not say who the attackers were, but said they were fought off before army reinforcements arrived.

  • In the years since 2015, there has been a marked increase in jihadists attacks in the country to a now almost-daily level. Some of these attacks have been linked to terror groups like Al-Qaeda and ISIS.
