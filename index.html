Brexit deadline looms over late talks.

    The Facts Today:

  • EU chief negotiator Michel Barnier had given PM Johnson until 12am Tuesday to agree to a border in the Irish Sea.

  • The DUP was to meet with the PM on Tuesday for 90 minutes, they said before the meeting that ‘further work is required’ on the issue of the Irish border.

  • MP’s are due to meet this Saturday - the final day that the UK had to request an extension from the EU.

  • EU leaders are to meet for an emergency summit on Thursday and Friday.

  • A deal for the EU27 to vote on is due to be published by No.10 today - talks on the deal continued through last night.

  • PM Johnson will require the support of Conservatives and the DUP to get his deal through Parliament.

Protests as Catalonian independence leaders jailed.

    The Facts Today:

  • Monday - 9 leaders of the Catalan independence movement were sentenced to between 9-13 years in prison on the grounds of sedition and misuse of public funds for their role in the October 2017 referendum.

  • All 9 have been banned from participating in public office.

  • Thousands of protesters converged on the regions airport, with the police using rubber bullets and batons. At least 75 have been injured.

  • Catalan News reported in November that 47.2% of Catalans are in favour of independence, whereas 43.2% were against.

Syrian Army and Russia move into Kurdish region.

    The Facts Today:

  • Thousands of Syrian Army troops have re-entered territory in the North East of Syria that had been in the control of Kurdish forces.

  • Russia announced on Tuesday that its troops were patrolling the region between the Syrian and Turkish forces.

  • This comes after US troops moved out of the region before a Turkish offensive into the Kurdish-controlled area.

  • Since the conflict in Syria escalated last week, UNICEF estimates ‘nearly 70,000’ children have been displaced(read more below).

IMF warns of slowing growth.

    The Facts Today:

  • The IMF has said that growth is expected at 3% in 2019, which is the lowest growth rate since 2008.

  • This can be compared to the growth rate in 2017, which was 2.5%

  • A recession is a growth rate of 2.5%.

  • The IMF estimates that if all scheduled US-China tariffs are in place, we could see a 0.8% fall in global economic growth.

  • Global growth should be lifted in 2020 due to emerging markets.

  • There is a wide variance in the world in terms of economic growth:China is expected to slow from 6.6% in 2018 to 6.1%. Germany is expected to slow from 1.5% to 0.5%. Britain is expected to slow from 1.4% to 1.2%

  • Gita Gopinath(IMF’s chief economist): “There is no room for policy mistakes and an urgent need for policymakers to cooperatively deescalate trade and geopolitical tensions”
