Last minute Brexit deal avoids hard border, European Council and Parliament to vote.

    The Facts Today:

  • The BBC have heard that there was to be ‘no deal tonight’[Wednesday].

  • European Council Donald Tusk: “The basic foundations of this agreement are ready and theoretically we could accept a deal”

  • Under these plans, Northern Ireland is to remain part of the UK customs, but also follow EU custom rules - as to avoid a hard border.

  • This comes the same day as a member of the New IRA spoke to Chanel 4 and said that any border infrastructure would be a ‘legitimate target for attack’.

  • Under the deal, Northern Ireland would have an option to reject or accept these plans.

  • The DUP still feels there are holes in this plan.

  • The deal(which does not yet have an official legal text) is to be voted on by the European Council on Thursday and Friday.

  • The British Parliament is to vote on the plan on Saturday.

Condemnation of removal of troops in America; Nuclear weapons left in Turkey.

    The Facts Today:

  • Around 50 Cold War nuclear bombs are now under the control of Turkish forces following the removal of American forces from their bases in the region. Turkey has no planes certified to carry nuclear weapons.

  • This comes as Congress voted 354-60 to condemn the withdrawal of troops.

  • Wednesday - President Trump: ‘I campaigned on bringing our soldiers back home, and that’s what I am doing’.

  • Turkish President Erdogan called on Kurdish fighters to ‘drop their weapons’, and leave the border region.

  • Vice-President Mike Pence is due to fly to Turkey on Wednesday to persuade Erdogan to end the military offensive on the Syria - Turkey border.

Votes being counted after Mozambique elections.

    The Facts Today:

  • Votes are being counted in Mozambique after presidential, parliamentary and provisional election.

  • The election is really between the ruling Frelimo party(who have ruled since independence from Portugal in 1975) and the anti-Communist rebels - turned political party Renamo.

  • This comes after a 15 year civil war(which really ended in 1992) came to a formal end in August. There has been a tense peace during the election - with violence occurring in the run up to it, last week an election observer was shot dead.

  • The current President Filipe Nyusi is expected to win - despite ongoing controversy since it was revealed in 2016 that the government secretly borrowed $2bn.

  • The population is 30m, and there are 13m registered to vote.

  • Incidents of ballot-stuffing were reported but the electoral commission denies any foul play.
