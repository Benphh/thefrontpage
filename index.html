62 dead after explosion in mosque in Afghanistan.

    The Facts Today:

  • An explosion during Friday prayers in a mosque in the Nangaher region has caused the death of 62 people - a spokesperson for a local governer has confirmed.

  • The explosion caused the roof on the mosque to collapse.

  • A presedential spokesperson has blamed the Taliban and told reporters it was a suicide bomber - although this is unconfirmed.

  • The Taliban have condemned the bombing calling it a 'major crime'.

The Brexit Withdrawal Agreement explained.

    The Facts Today:

  • Under the Agreement, Northern Ireland will obey some EU rules like laws for vetinary controls, agricultural production and VAT on goods. These measures mean there is nor border on the island of Ireland, and instead custom checks will be at the Irish Sea.

  • The agreement will include a mechanism for multi-party consent on the issue from Stormont after two years.

  • PM Johnson has said that his aim is for an eventual free trade agreement with Europe, with no tariffs - something Britain had inside the EU.

  • The Labour Party are opposed to the bill on the basis that enviromental and worker protections have been moved to the political declaration of the agreement - which can be thought of as future intentions, but not the current rules.

Voting on Brexit tomorrow explained.

    The Facts Today:

  • The Parliament contains 650 votes in total. This includes the Speaker and his 3 deputies, and the 7 Sinn Fein memebers. This leaves 639 votes, meaning PM Johnson needs 320 votes to pass his deal.

  • The DUP, SNP and Labour have all come out against it - citing concerns over the isolation of Northern Ireland from Britain, the lack of acknowledgemnt of Scotish Remain support and the devaluation of environmental and worker protections, respectively.

  • Some from Labour are expected to rebel against party policy, and most of the 21 Conservatives that were expelled from the party are expected to vote for the deal.

  • Predictions are placing For and Against at a roughly equal footing with around 300 votes , with over 30 votes to play for.
